Liens That Survive Bankruptcy Discharge

A bankruptcy discharge eliminates personal liability but does not automatically remove liens. Understanding this distinction is essential.

Home›What Liens Survive Bankruptcy Discharge? | bankruptcylien.com›Liens That Survive Bankruptcy Discharge

The Fundamental Rule

The bankruptcy discharge eliminates your personal obligation to pay a debt. It does not automatically eliminate a creditor's lien on your property. This is the most important concept in understanding liens in bankruptcy.

Dewsnup v. Timm, 502 U.S. 410 (1992): The Supreme Court held that liens pass through bankruptcy unaffected by the discharge unless the debtor takes specific steps to avoid, strip, or otherwise address them.

Practical Consequences

After a Chapter 7 discharge with surviving liens:

  • Mortgage: The lender cannot sue you for a deficiency. But if you stop paying, they can foreclose on the lien. If you want to keep the house, you must keep paying.
  • Car loan: Same principle. The lender cannot sue you personally, but can repossess the car if you default.
  • Judgment lien: If not avoided through 522(f), the lien remains on the property even though the underlying judgment debt is discharged.
  • Tax lien: Survives discharge. The IRS or state can enforce the lien against property, even though personal liability may have been eliminated.

Strategic Implications

Understanding which liens survive affects your choice of chapter:

  • Chapter 7: Fast discharge, but almost no lien modification tools (only 522(f) avoidance and 722 redemption)
  • Chapter 13: Slower process, but powerful lien tools (stripping, cramdown, cure and maintain)

If you have significant liens on your property - especially underwater junior mortgages or judgment liens - Chapter 13 may be the better choice even though it requires years of plan payments.

Get advice before filing. The decision about which liens to address and how to address them should be made before filing, not after. A competent bankruptcy attorney can evaluate your liens and recommend the best strategy.
